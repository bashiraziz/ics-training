    q: "What is a novation agreement and when is it required?",
    a: "A novation agreement under FAR 42.1204 is a three-party agreement (old contractor, new contractor/successor, and government) formally recognizing the successor as the new contract party after a transfer of assets. It is required when a government contractor's assets transfer to a successor through a sale, merger, or reorganization."
  },
  {
    q: "What happens to open ICS years when a novation agreement is executed?",
    a: "Open ICS years transfer to the successor. The novation agreement explicitly makes the successor responsible for all contract obligations including: filing ICS for completed fiscal years, cooperating with DCAA audits of those years, and repaying any amounts disallowed as a result of those audits."
  },
  {
    q: "What are the five key ICS-specific items an acquirer should examine during due diligence?",
    a: "1) How many ICS years are open. 2) Known DCAA audit findings and the status of any management responses or COFDs. 3) Variance between provisional billing rates and actual costs. 4) Presence of historically unallowable cost categories in G&A or overhead. 5) Whether the CAS Disclosure Statement is current and consistent with actual practices."
  },
  {
    q: "What M&A deal protections are typically used to address open ICS year risk?",
    a: "Escrow holdbacks (typically 5-15% of purchase price, held 3-5 years pending audit resolution); seller indemnification for disallowances exceeding a threshold; and representations and warranties about the accuracy of ICS filings and absence of known unallowable costs."
  },
  {
    q: "Why does corporate M&A activity typically require CAS Disclosure Statement amendments?",
    a: "Because the combined entity's cost accounting practices often differ from either predecessor — segment definitions change, new cost pools are created or eliminated, and allocation bases shift. Failure to amend the DS after a restructuring is itself a CAS noncompliance."
  },
  {
    q: "What must a contractor do if a business segment is divested?",
    a: "Unwind any ICS submissions that combined the divested segment's costs with retained segments. Allocate historical home office costs between segments, determine contract assignments, and possibly file amended ICS submissions. The ACO must be engaged to address how open years that include both segments' costs will be handled."
  },
  {
    q: "Why is the government not required to approve a novation agreement?",
    a: "The government evaluates whether recognizing the successor is in the government's interest. If the successor lacks the financial capability, clearances, or technical capability to perform, the government may decline novation — leaving the contracts subject to termination or other action."
  }

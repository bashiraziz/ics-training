<FlashcardDeck cards={[
  {
    q: "What is the CAS Disclosure Statement and why does it matter for ICS?",
    a: "The Disclosure Statement (Form CASB DS-1) is the contractor's written description of its cost accounting practices — how costs are accumulated, classified, and allocated. DCAA compares it to your ICS during audit. Any discrepancy between the DS and your actual ICS practices constitutes a CAS noncompliance."
  },
  {
    q: "What triggers full CAS coverage requiring a Disclosure Statement?",
    a: "Full CAS coverage is triggered when a contractor's CAS-covered contract awards exceed $50 million in the prior cost accounting period. Modified CAS coverage (only CAS 401, 402, 405, 406) applies when a single CAS-covered contract is $2 million or more but full coverage is not yet triggered."
  },
  {
    q: "Which three sections of the DS-1 are most directly relevant to ICS?",
    a: "Section 3 (Indirect Costs) — describes pools and allocation bases. Section 4 (Depreciation and Capital Expenditures) — describes asset depreciation methods. Section 6 (Deferred Compensation and Insurance) — describes pension cost measurement, which must match the ICS figures."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the consequence of a CAS noncompliance finding?",
    a: "A mandatory price adjustment on all affected CAS-covered contracts, calculated as the difference between costs computed under the disclosed practice and costs as actually incurred. The adjustment can be in the government's favor or the contractor's, depending on which method produces higher costs."
  },
  {
    q: "When must a contractor file a DS amendment — before or after changing a practice?",
    a: "For required changes (e.g., changes to comply with a new CAS standard): the amendment and price adjustment must be agreed before implementation. For unilateral changes: the contractor must notify the ACO in advance; if implemented without prior approval, a retroactive price adjustment is required."
  },
  {
    q: "What is a common DS-ICS discrepancy DCAA finds during adequacy review?",
    a: "Allocation base mismatch: the DS says G&A is allocated on Total Cost Input, but the ICS uses a modified TCI that excludes cost categories not mentioned in the DS. Also common: costs moved between pools (e.g., from overhead to G&A) without a DS amendment."
  },
  {
    q: "What should a contractor do if it discovers its DS is out of date before filing ICS?",
    a: "File a DS amendment immediately. Notify the ACO in writing of the change, compute the cost impact on CAS-covered contracts, and disclose the effective date of the change. Proactive disclosure is far better than DCAA discovering an undisclosed change during audit."
  }
]} />

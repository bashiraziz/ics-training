    q: "How long does DCAA have to determine whether an ICS submission is adequate?",
    a: "60 days from receipt. If DCAA issues a deficiency letter (inadequate determination), the contractor must correct and resubmit, and the 60-day clock restarts. The 6-month filing window also pauses during the period the submission is deemed inadequate."
  },
  {
    q: "What does DCAA's adequacy review check for?",
    a: "Whether the submission is complete enough to audit — all required schedules present, indirect pools and bases identified, reconciliation to financial statements included, and certification signed by an authorized official. Adequacy is about completeness, not accuracy."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the purpose of the desk review phase?",
    a: "The auditor reviews the ICS without visiting the contractor to check mathematical accuracy, internal consistency between schedules, year-over-year rate fluctuations, comparison of actual to provisional billing rates, and initial identification of potential unallowable cost areas. Information requests (IRs) are issued during this phase."
  },
  {
    q: "How does DCAA select which costs and contracts to audit in detail?",
    a: "DCAA uses risk-based sampling. High-risk cost categories (travel, consultants, entertainment, compensation, bonuses) and high-value contracts receive more attention. Prior year findings in a specific category increase scrutiny of that category in subsequent years."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the contractor's opportunity after receiving the draft audit report?",
    a: "The contractor submits a written management response — a formal rebuttal addressing each finding with regulatory citations and documentary evidence. A strong response can cause DCAA to reduce or withdraw findings before the final report is issued to the ACO."
  },
  {
    q: "Who has the authority to establish final indirect cost rates — DCAA or the ACO?",
    a: "The ACO (Administrative Contracting Officer). DCAA's audit report is a recommendation; the ACO can accept it, reject it, or negotiate a different outcome. Contractors should engage the ACO as a separate decision-maker and not treat DCAA's findings as the final word."
  },
  {
    q: "What happens after final indirect cost rates are established by the ACO?",
    a: "Contractors submit final invoices on cost-type contracts. Overbilled amounts are repaid with CDA interest. Underbilled amounts are collected. Contracts eligible for closeout proceed to formal closeout. The ICS year is considered closed."
  },
  {
    q: "Why do DCAA ICS audits typically take 3-7 years from submission to final rates?",
    a: "Each phase takes significant time: adequacy and desk review (months), document requests and field testing (6-18 months), draft and final report cycles (3-9 months), and rate negotiation (3-12 months). Contractor delays in responding to IRs and DCAA workload backlogs extend timelines further."
  }

<Step moduleId="module7" stepId="1" title="Step 1: Conduct ICS Due Diligence">
  <p>
    Orion Federal Group is acquiring SkyBridge Systems, a 180-person government contractor with $28M in annual revenue, for a proposed purchase price of $22M. Orion's deal team has been granted data room access. The following ICS-related facts have been identified:
  </p>
  <p>
    - SkyBridge has four open ICS years (Years 1 through 4).<br/>
    - Year 1 has a final DCAA audit report with $210,000 in disallowed costs. SkyBridge filed a management response but the ACO has not issued a COFD.<br/>
    - Year 2 was submitted on time but received an inadequacy determination 14 months ago. SkyBridge has not corrected and resubmitted.<br/>
    - Years 3 and 4 have been submitted and deemed adequate; no audit activity yet.<br/>
    - SkyBridge's Disclosure Statement was last updated 5 years ago. In the past 2 years, SkyBridge changed its G&A allocation base from TCI to Value Added without filing a DS amendment.<br/>
    - Provisional billing rates for all four years show SkyBridge billed G&A at rates between 8% and 12% above actual.
  </p>
  <Question answer="The ICS exposure items are: (1) Year 1: $210,000 DCAA-disallowed costs. The ACO has not issued a COFD, so negotiation may reduce this, but the floor is the disallowed amount if the ACO accepts DCAA fully. Budget at least $210,000. (2) Year 2: The 14-month-old inadequacy determination with no correction is a serious problem. The year is not yet in audit. Orion will inherit the obligation to correct and resubmit, and then face audit. This is an unknown exposure — could be $0 or significant depending on what DCAA finds. Conservative reserve: $100,000-$200,000. (3) Years 3 and 4: Submitted and adequate, no findings yet. Reserve conservatively based on the $8-12% overbilling of G&A — on estimated $10M in direct costs per year, an 8% G&A rate excess = $800,000/year overbilled. Total potential overbilling repayment for all four years: substantial. (4) CAS noncompliance: 2 years of undisclosed G&A base change. Retroactive price adjustment on all CAS-covered contracts for two years. Rough estimate: if the change from TCI to Value Added affected $15M in contract costs at a 1.5% rate difference, the impact is ~$225,000. Total risk range: $735,000 to $1.5M+ depending on audit outcomes.">
    Quantify the ICS exposure for each open year and the CAS noncompliance. What is the realistic risk range Orion is acquiring?
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Orion should negotiate: (1) An escrow holdback of 8-12% of the $22M purchase price — approximately $1.76M to $2.64M — held for 4-5 years pending resolution of all four open ICS years. The escrow is released to SkyBridge's sellers as each year closes without material disallowance above an agreed threshold. (2) An indemnification clause: the selling shareholders indemnify Orion for any disallowances, overbilling repayments, or CAS price adjustments exceeding a basket (e.g., the first $150,000 is Orion's risk; anything above that is the sellers' responsibility). (3) Representations and warranties: sellers represent that no other DCAA findings exist, the financial statements accurately reflect ICS-related liabilities, and no material unallowable costs are known to exist beyond disclosed items. (4) A specific obligation: sellers cooperate post-closing to provide records needed for DCAA audit of open years, since the sellers' personnel have the institutional knowledge about those years' costs.">
    What specific deal protections should Orion negotiate to address the ICS exposure? Name the structure, the suggested amount, and the duration for each protection.
  </Question>
</Step>

<Step moduleId="module7" stepId="2" title="Step 2: Manage the Novation Process">
  <p>
    The acquisition closes on September 30. SkyBridge's assets are transferred to Orion Federal Group. SkyBridge has 12 active government contracts. Orion wants to continue performing on all 12.
  </p>
  <Question answer="Orion must submit a novation agreement package to the cognizant ACO (or to each ACO for contracts administered by different offices) under FAR 42.1204. The package must include: (1) a written request for novation; (2) the asset purchase agreement or merger documents showing the transfer; (3) a list of all contracts to be novated (by contract number, agency, and value); (4) financial statements of Orion Federal Group demonstrating its financial capability to perform; (5) evidence of legal authority to conduct the acquisition; (6) evidence that Orion holds or can obtain the necessary security clearances, facilities clearances, and technical capabilities; (7) a statement that Orion assumes all obligations under the listed contracts, including open ICS years. Until the novation agreement is approved by the government, Orion cannot be recognized as the contract party of record. SkyBridge (the old entity) technically remains the contract party, which creates legal and operational complications for every invoice Orion submits.">
    What must Orion submit to the government to obtain a novation agreement, and what risk does Orion face if it begins performing on contracts before novation is approved?
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Open ICS years transfer to Orion under the novation agreement. Specifically: Orion becomes responsible for filing any ICS not yet filed (e.g., if Year 5 comes due after the novation closes), correcting Year 2's inadequacy deficiency and resubmitting, cooperating with DCAA audits of Years 1-4, and repaying any amounts disallowed as a result of those audits. The novation does not reset the clock or create a fresh start — the government retains all audit and disallowance rights for the open years as if SkyBridge had never been acquired. Orion's practical obligation: immediately assign an experienced government contracts accounting team to get Year 2 corrected and resubmitted (now 14+ months overdue), and engage proactively with DCAA on the status of Years 3 and 4.">
    What specific ICS obligations does Orion assume under the novation agreement for SkyBridge's four open years? What immediate action should Orion take after novation is approved?
  </Question>
</Step>

<Step moduleId="module7" stepId="3" title="Step 3: Address the CAS Disclosure Statement Gap">
  <p>
    Post-acquisition, Orion's Chief Compliance Officer conducts a DS gap analysis. She confirms: SkyBridge's DS (Section 3) says G&A is allocated on TCI. For the past 2 years, SkyBridge actually used Value Added. Orion now wants to operate SkyBridge as a segment using Value Added going forward — the same base Orion's other segment uses.
  </p>
  <Question answer="SkyBridge (now an Orion segment) has a CAS noncompliance for the two years it used Value Added without a DS amendment. Orion must: (1) Notify the ACO in writing of the noncompliance, identifying the effective date the practice changed and the period of noncompliance. (2) Compute the cost impact on all CAS-covered contracts for those two years — comparing costs under the disclosed TCI method versus the actual Value Added method. (3) Work with the ACO to agree on price adjustments. If Value Added produced a higher G&A rate (because a smaller base was used), the government was overcharged — Orion owes a credit. If Value Added produced a lower rate, the contractor is owed additional payment. (4) File a DS amendment for the SkyBridge segment describing Value Added as the G&A allocation base, with the corrected effective date. Proactive disclosure after discovery (even post-acquisition) is treated more favorably than DCAA discovering the noncompliance during audit.">
    What must Orion do to address the two-year CAS noncompliance it inherited? List the steps in order and explain the financial consequence.
  </Question>
  <Question answer="To align SkyBridge's G&A base with Orion's other segment on a going-forward basis, Orion must file a DS amendment for the SkyBridge segment changing Section 3's G&A allocation base description to Value Added, effective the start of the current fiscal year (or the date of the acquisition, if Orion wants alignment from the acquisition date). Because this is a voluntary change, Orion must: notify the ACO in advance of implementation; compute the cost impact comparing the old TCI method to the new Value Added method for all existing CAS-covered contracts; and agree on any required price adjustment. Importantly, this amendment covers the going-forward practice; it does not retroactively cure the prior two-year noncompliance — those years need separate remediation as described in the previous question. Orion should file both the retroactive correction and the prospective amendment as a coordinated package with the ACO to resolve the full issue cleanly.">
    How should Orion handle the going-forward DS amendment to align SkyBridge to Value Added — and how does that differ from addressing the retroactive noncompliance period?
  </Question>
</Step>

    q: "Which FAR clause creates the obligation to submit an incurred cost proposal, and what contract types include it?",
    a: "FAR 52.216-7, 'Allowable Cost and Payment.' It is incorporated into cost-reimbursable contracts (CPFF, CPAF, CPIF) and time-and-materials contracts. It requires the contractor to submit a final indirect cost rate proposal within six months of fiscal year-end."

  {
    q: "What is the difference between a provisional billing rate and a final indirect cost rate?",
    a: "A provisional rate is an estimated rate used during the year so the contractor can bill without waiting for year-end actuals. A final rate is computed from actual year-end costs in the ICS. The difference between the two, applied to the actual allocation base, determines whether the contractor overbilled or underbilled the government."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the role of the Administrative Contracting Officer (ACO) in the ICS process?",
    a: "The ACO administers the contract after award, receives the ICS submission, coordinates with DCAA during the audit, and ultimately negotiates and executes the final indirect rate agreement with the contractor. For most DoD contractors, the ACO is stationed at DCMA."
  },
  {
    q: "What does FAR 42.705 govern?",
    a: "FAR 42.705 governs the determination of final indirect cost rates. It gives the cognizant federal agency (DCAA for audit, ACO for rate negotiation) the authority to establish final rates either by agreement with the contractor or through a unilateral determination. Final rates established under FAR 42.705 apply to all contracts subject to FAR 52.216-7 for that fiscal year."
  },
  {
    q: "What are the five criteria a cost must meet to be allowable under FAR 31.201-2?",
    a: "The cost must be (1) reasonable — a prudent person would incur it; (2) allocable — it benefits the contract or can be fairly distributed; (3) in compliance with applicable Cost Accounting Standards or GAAP; (4) not excluded by specific contract terms; and (5) not expressly unallowable under FAR Part 31 cost principles."
  },
  {
    q: "What happens if a contractor misses the six-month ICS filing deadline?",
    a: "The ACO may make a unilateral determination of final indirect cost rates, which is rarely favorable to the contractor. Missing the deadline also signals compliance weakness to DCAA and can jeopardize the contractor's ability to negotiate favorable rates. There is no automatic extension."
  },
  {
    q: "What does FAR 42.703 (Basic Agreement on Indirect Cost Rates) do?",
    a: "FAR 42.703 allows the contracting officer and contractor to establish a written agreement on the contractor's indirect cost rate structure — which pools exist, how they are defined, and what allocation bases they use. The ICS must be consistent with this agreed structure. Changes to pool definitions must be explained and, ideally, negotiated in advance."
  },
  {
    q: "Describe the ICS lifecycle from fiscal year-end to audit closure.",
    a: "Year-end → ICS filed within 6 months → DCAA adequacy review (30–90 days, may result in deficiency letter) → DCAA audit (desk review or field audit) → DCAA report to ACO → ACO negotiates final rates → rate agreement executed → billing adjustments processed → contracts closed for that year. Total elapsed time is typically 18 months to 4 years."

  {
    q: "What is Schedule A in the ICS, and what does each row represent?",
    a: "Schedule A is the Summary of Claimed Indirect Costs — the top-level view of the entire indirect cost claim. Each row represents one indirect cost pool (e.g., Fringe Benefits, Overhead, G&A). It shows total costs in the pool, less unallowable costs, equals allowable costs claimed, divided by the allocation base, which produces the claimed rate."
  },
  {
    q: "How is the claimed indirect cost rate computed from Schedule A data?",
    a: "Claimed Rate = Allowable Costs Claimed ÷ Allocation Base. For example, if allowable costs in the Overhead pool are $2,053,000 and the direct labor allocation base is $3,200,000, the claimed Overhead rate is 64.2%."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the most critical reconciliation requirement for Schedule A?",
    a: "The 'Allowable Costs Claimed' total for each pool in Schedule A must exactly equal the pool total shown in Schedule B for that same pool. Any discrepancy between A and B — even a small one — is grounds for a DCAA deficiency finding."
  },
  {
    q: "Name three common Schedule A errors.",
    a: "(1) Unallowable costs included in pool totals without a deduction in the 'Less Unallowable' column, inflating the claimed rate. (2) Pool totals that don't reconcile to Schedule B detail, creating an unexplained gap. (3) Using a wrong or budgeted allocation base instead of actual year-end figures, producing an incorrect rate."
  },
  {
    q: "Where do the allocation base totals in Schedule A come from?",
    a: "The allocation base totals must come from actual year-end data — typically Schedule C (direct costs by contract) for direct labor and total direct costs. Using budgeted or prior-year base figures instead of actual year-end actuals is a common error that produces incorrect rates and may trigger an adequacy finding."
  },
  {
    q: "A contractor's Fringe Benefits pool has total booked costs of $1,250,000. It identifies $8,000 in unallowable costs. The total labor allocation base is $4,140,000. What is the claimed Fringe rate?",
    a: "Allowable costs = $1,250,000 − $8,000 = $1,242,000. Claimed rate = $1,242,000 ÷ $4,140,000 = 30.0%."
  },
  {
    q: "How does Schedule A relate to Schedule D?",
    a: "The 'Claimed Rate' from Schedule A becomes the 'Final Rate' column in Schedule D. Schedule D then compares the final rate to the provisional billing rate used during the year, computes the variance, and multiplies it by the actual allocation base to determine the dollar amount of any overbilling or underbilling."
  }

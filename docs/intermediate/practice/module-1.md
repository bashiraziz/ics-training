<Step moduleId="module1" stepId="1" title="Step 1: Identifying the Obligation">
  <p>Apex Solutions is a 40-person consulting firm that performs both commercial and government work. It has the following contracts currently active:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Contract A: Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee with the Army — $2.1M ceiling, FAR 52.216-7 is in the contract</li>
    <li>Contract B: Firm-Fixed-Price with the Navy — $800K, no cost reporting requirements</li>
    <li>Contract C: Time-and-Materials with the Air Force — $1.4M ceiling, FAR 52.216-7 is in the contract</li>
    <li>Contract D: Commercial services contract — no government nexus</li>
  </ul>
  <Question answer="Contracts A and C require an ICS submission. Both contain FAR 52.216-7, the 'Allowable Cost and Payment' clause. Contract B is a firm-fixed-price contract, so the government has no right to audit or adjust indirect costs — no ICS is required. Contract D is commercial with no government cost-reimbursable obligation.">
    Which of Apex's contracts require an ICS filing, and why?
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Apex must file within six months of its fiscal year-end. For a calendar-year contractor, that means by June 30. The six-month period is counted from the last day of the fiscal year in which the cost-reimbursable or T&M contract was active — so if the fiscal year ended December 31, the deadline is June 30 of the following year.">
    If Apex's fiscal year ends December 31, when must its ICS be filed?
  </Question>
</Step>

<Step moduleId="module1" stepId="2" title="Step 2: ACO vs. PCO Roles">
  <p>Apex has received a notification from DCMA that a new Administrative Contracting Officer (ACO) has been assigned. The company's Controller is unsure whether to contact the ACO or the Procuring Contracting Officer (PCO) for the following situations:</p>
  <ol>
    <li>Apex wants to discuss the provisional billing rates for next year.</li>
    <li>Apex has a question about a contract performance requirement in Contract A's Statement of Work.</li>
    <li>DCAA issued a letter requesting clarification on last year's ICS submission.</li>
  </ol>
  <Question answer="Situation 1 (provisional rates) → ACO. Rate negotiations, ICS matters, and billing adjustments are all ACO functions. Situation 2 (SOW performance) → PCO. The PCO awarded the contract and retains authority over its terms and performance requirements. Situation 3 (DCAA clarification) → ACO receives copies of all DCAA correspondence, and the ACO coordinates between DCAA and the contractor. Apex should respond directly to DCAA with a copy to the ACO.">
    For each situation, who is the correct point of contact — ACO or PCO? Explain each.
  </Question>
  <Question answer="The ACO is responsible for: administering contracts post-award, receiving and processing the ICS submission, coordinating with DCAA during the audit, negotiating final indirect cost rates under FAR 42.705, executing the final rate agreement, and processing billing adjustments. The ACO does not have authority over the original contract's scope or price — that is the PCO's domain.">
    List four specific ICS-related responsibilities that belong to the ACO.
  </Question>
</Step>

<Step moduleId="module1" stepId="3" title="Step 3: Provisional vs. Final Rates">
  <p>At the start of its fiscal year, Apex negotiated the following provisional billing rates with the ACO:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Fringe Benefits: 29%</li>
    <li>Overhead: 55%</li>
    <li>G&A: 12%</li>
  </ul>
  <p>After year-end, Apex's ICS shows final rates of Fringe 31%, Overhead 52%, G&A 13%. During the year, Apex billed $600,000 in direct labor on cost-type contracts.</p>
  <Question answer="For each pool, compare final vs. provisional rate and multiply by the relevant base. Assuming the direct labor base applies to Overhead and Fringe, and a TCI base applies to G&A: Fringe: (31% − 29%) × $600,000 = +$12,000 underbilled. Overhead: (52% − 55%) × $600,000 = −$18,000 overbilled. G&A: (13% − 12%) × base — we need the TCI base, but if we assume TCI is approximately $1,400,000, G&A variance = +$14,000 underbilled. Net depends on the G&A base, but Overhead overbilling partially offsets fringe and G&A underbilling.">
    Identify which pools were overbilled and which were underbilled, and explain the direction of the required adjustment for each.
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Billing adjustments cannot be processed until final rates are established by a rate agreement or ACO unilateral determination. Even though the ICS shows the final rates, those rates are not 'established' until DCAA completes its audit, issues a report to the ACO, and the ACO either negotiates an agreement with Apex or issues a unilateral determination. This process typically takes 18–36 months after the ICS is filed.">
    Can Apex submit a final invoice for its underbilling immediately after filing the ICS? Why or why not?
  </Question>
</Step>

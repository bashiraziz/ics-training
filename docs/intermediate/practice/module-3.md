<Step moduleId="module3" stepId="1" title="Step 1: Building a Schedule B Section">
  <p>Cascade Systems has the following costs booked to its G&A pool for the fiscal year:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Executive salaries: $520,000</li>
    <li>Accounting/finance staff: $180,000</li>
    <li>Legal fees: $45,000 (all allowable)</li>
    <li>Business development salaries: $95,000</li>
    <li>Charitable contribution to local food bank: $8,000</li>
    <li>Corporate facility rent: $110,000</li>
    <li>Office supplies and miscellaneous: $22,000</li>
    <li>Interest expense on line of credit: $14,000</li>
  </ul>
  <Question answer="Unallowable costs: Charitable contribution $8,000 (FAR 31.205-8) and Interest expense $14,000 (FAR 31.205-20). Total unallowable: $22,000. The Schedule B G&A section should list all eight cost elements with totals, then show a deduction of $22,000 in the unallowable column, producing allowable claimed costs of $972,000. Each unallowable line should reference the applicable FAR citation.">
    Identify all unallowable costs in the G&A pool, cite the applicable FAR reference for each, and describe how they should appear on Schedule B.
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Total G&A pool = $520,000 + $180,000 + $45,000 + $95,000 + $8,000 + $110,000 + $22,000 + $14,000 = $994,000. Less unallowable = $22,000. Allowable claimed = $972,000. This $972,000 is the figure that flows to the G&A row in Schedule A as 'Allowable Costs Claimed.'">
    Calculate the allowable G&A costs that will flow to Schedule A. Show your arithmetic.
  </Question>
</Step>

<Step moduleId="module3" stepId="2" title="Step 2: Choosing the Right Allocation Base">
  <p>Cascade Systems is deciding which G&A allocation base to use. Here is its cost structure for the year:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Direct labor: $3,100,000</li>
    <li>Direct materials: $2,800,000 (all subcontracted — pass-through)</li>
    <li>Other direct costs: $250,000</li>
    <li>Allocated overhead: $1,860,000</li>
    <li>Total subcontract pass-through costs: $2,800,000</li>
  </ul>
  <Question answer="Under TCI base: $3,100,000 + $2,800,000 + $250,000 + $1,860,000 = $8,010,000. G&A rate = $972,000 / $8,010,000 = 12.1%. Under Value-Added (TCI minus subcontracts/materials): $8,010,000 − $2,800,000 = $5,210,000. G&A rate = $972,000 / $5,210,000 = 18.7%. The VA base produces a higher rate but a more equitable allocation because it removes the large pass-through subcontract costs from the base. Pass-through subcontracts require little G&A management effort, so including them in the base dilutes the rate unfairly.">
    Calculate the G&A rate under both Total Cost Input (TCI) and Value-Added (VA) bases. Which base produces the higher rate, and which is more equitable given Cascade's cost structure?
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Cascade must use whichever base is consistent with its disclosed accounting practices (its accounting manual, prior ICS submissions, or any negotiated rate agreement under FAR 42.703). It cannot switch bases from year to year without notifying the ACO and providing justification. If no base has been established, either TCI or VA is acceptable — but once chosen, it should be applied consistently. Given the large subcontract pass-through, a value-added base would be the more defensible choice if challenged.">
    Can Cascade freely choose either base? What governs the selection?
  </Question>
</Step>

<Step moduleId="module3" stepId="3" title="Step 3: Fringe Benefits Pool and Allocation">
  <p>Cascade's Fringe Benefits pool for the year contains:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>FICA taxes: $237,000</li>
    <li>Health insurance premiums: $310,000</li>
    <li>Retirement plan contributions: $120,000</li>
    <li>Workers' compensation insurance: $48,000</li>
    <li>Paid time off accrual: $95,000</li>
    <li>Holiday party expenses: $18,000</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Total direct labor during the year: $3,100,000. Total indirect labor: $1,240,000.</p>
  <Question answer="Holiday party expenses of $18,000 are unallowable under FAR 31.205-14 (entertainment). All other costs are standard fringe benefit items allowable under FAR 31.205-6. Allowable Fringe pool = $237,000 + $310,000 + $120,000 + $48,000 + $95,000 = $810,000. Total incurred = $828,000.">
    Identify any unallowable costs in the Fringe pool and compute the allowable pool total.
  </Question>
  <Question answer="Fringe allocation base = total labor dollars = $3,100,000 (direct) + $1,240,000 (indirect) = $4,340,000. Fringe rate = $810,000 / $4,340,000 = 18.7%. The total labor base is appropriate because fringe costs (health insurance, retirement, etc.) benefit all employees equally — both direct and indirect — so it is equitable to spread them across all labor.">
    Compute the Fringe Benefits rate. Explain why total labor dollars (direct + indirect) is the appropriate allocation base.
  </Question>
</Step>

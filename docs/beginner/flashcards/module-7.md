<FlashcardDeck cards={[
  {
    q: "What is DCAA and what authority does it have over contracts?",
    a: "DCAA (Defense Contract Audit Agency) is an independent DoD agency that audits contractor costs. It has no contract authority — it cannot approve, reject, or modify contracts. It audits costs and provides findings and recommendations to contracting officers, who make final decisions."
  },
  {
    q: "What is DCAA's adequacy review, and what triggers it?",
    a: "The adequacy review (desk review) is DCAA's first action after receiving an ICS. It checks whether all required schedules are present, the certification is signed, rates can be computed, and data is internally consistent. An inadequate submission is returned and must be refiled."
  },
  {
    q: "What are the three main paths after DCAA confirms adequacy?",
    a: "1) No further audit action (low-risk, small dollar, strong track record). 2) Limited-scope audit (focused on specific risk areas). 3) Full incurred cost audit (comprehensive review of all schedules and documentation)."
  },
  {
    q: "What four things does DCAA consistently test in any ICS audit?",
    a: "Mathematical accuracy (schedules compute correctly), proper cost classification (direct vs. indirect), exclusion of unallowable costs from claimed pools, and adequacy of supporting documentation for all claimed costs."
  },
  {
    q: "Name three common audit triggers that increase DCAA scrutiny.",
    a: "Large rate variances year-over-year without explanation, missing or improperly signed Schedule H certification, and large claimed amounts in accounts with sparse supporting documentation."
  },
  {
    q: "After DCAA issues a draft audit report with findings, what can the contractor do?",
    a: "The contractor can accept the findings, provide additional documentation to resolve questioned costs, or formally disagree with DCAA's position. The contractor's written response is considered before DCAA issues its final audit report."
  },
  {
    q: "Who makes the final determination on disallowed costs — DCAA or the Contracting Officer?",
    a: "The Contracting Officer. DCAA issues findings and recommendations in its audit report, but the CO has authority to accept, modify, or reject those recommendations and makes the binding determination on final rates and cost allowability."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the best approach when DCAA questions a cost you believe is allowable?",
    a: "Respond in writing with the specific FAR citation (typically from FAR Part 31.205) that supports the cost's allowability, along with any supporting documentation. A well-cited, documented response resolves many questions without formal disallowance."
  }
]} />

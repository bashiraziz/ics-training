<FlashcardDeck cards={[
  {
    q: "Why do provisional rates exist if final rates will replace them?",
    a: "Because contractors must invoice the government throughout the year before actual costs are known. Provisional rates are pre-approved estimates that allow billing to proceed while final rates are calculated after year-end."
  },
  {
    q: "How are provisional billing rates established?",
    a: "Either through negotiation with DCAA or the Contracting Officer before the fiscal year begins, or by carrying forward prior-year actual rates when no formal negotiation takes place."
  },
  {
    q: "What is a rate variance?",
    a: "The difference between the provisional billing rate used during the year and the final indirect rate computed from actual costs after year-end. Variances result in either overbillings (contractor owes refund) or underbillings (contractor can invoice more)."
  },
  {
    q: "If provisional overhead was 85% and the final rate is 78%, who owes money and how much per $1,000 of direct labor?",
    a: "The contractor owes the government money. The contractor billed $850 in overhead per $1,000 of direct labor but only earned $780. The overbilling is $70 per $1,000 of direct labor billed."
  },
  {
    q: "What does 'true-up' mean in the context of the ICS?",
    a: "The true-up is the billing adjustment process after final rates are established. Overbillings are refunded or credited; underbillings are invoiced. It settles the difference between provisional and final rates on a contract-by-contract basis."
  },
  {
    q: "Once final rates are established for a fiscal year, can they be changed?",
    a: "No. Final rates are permanently fixed for the fiscal year they cover. They cannot be revised later except in cases of fraud or clerical error."
  },
  {
    q: "Who processes the actual money settlement after final rates are agreed upon?",
    a: "The Contracting Officer (CO) — not DCAA. DCAA establishes or recommends the rates; the CO handles the actual billing adjustments, refund demands, and underbilling invoices."
  }
]} />

  {
    q: "What does Schedule A contain and who primarily uses it?",
    a: "Schedule A contains a summary of each indirect cost pool — total claimed costs, the allocation base, and the resulting final indirect rate. DCAA auditors use it as the starting point for rate verification; COs use it to understand the big picture before approving final rates."
  },
  {
    q: "What does Schedule B contain, and why is it a primary audit document?",
    a: "Schedule B contains a line-by-line listing of every cost element within each indirect pool. It is the primary audit document because DCAA traces each cost back to the general ledger and source documents (invoices, payroll records) to verify allowability."
  },
  {
    q: "What does Schedule C contain?",
    a: "Schedule C contains direct costs listed by contract — direct labor, materials, subcontractor costs, travel, and other direct costs for each individual government contract active during the fiscal year."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the purpose of Schedule D?",
    a: "Schedule D provides a side-by-side comparison of provisional billing rates and final indirect rates for each pool. It formally documents rate variances and is the primary rate settlement document used by the Contracting Officer and DCAA."
  },
  {
    q: "What is Schedule E and what does it determine?",
    a: "Schedule E is the reconciliation of claimed vs. billed costs by contract. For each contract, it shows costs billed (provisional rates), costs claimed (final rates), and the net variance. It determines who owes whom money after final rates are established."
  },
  {
    q: "What is Schedule H and why is it legally critical?",
    a: "Schedule H is the contractor certification — a signed statement by a senior company official certifying the ICS is accurate and complete. It is legally critical because false statements on a certified ICS can trigger False Claims Act liability. An unsigned Schedule H renders the submission inadequate."
  },
  {
    q: "If a contractor has no subcontracts, does it need to submit Schedule F?",
    a: "Schedule F covers subcontract information and is only applicable when the contractor has subcontracts under its government prime contracts. If there are no subcontracts, this schedule is typically marked not applicable."
  },
  {
    q: "What happens if any required schedule is missing from the ICS?",
    a: "DCAA will find the submission inadequate during the desk review, return it with a deficiency notice, and the contractor must refile. The original submission date no longer counts, which can put the contractor past the filing deadline."
  }

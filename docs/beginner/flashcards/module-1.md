<FlashcardDeck cards={[
  {
    q: "What does ICS stand for, and what is it?",
    a: "ICS stands for Incurred Cost Submission. It is the annual package a government contractor submits to DCAA reconciling actual indirect costs incurred during the fiscal year against the provisional billing rates used during the year."
  },
  {
    q: "What FAR clause requires contractors to submit an ICS?",
    a: "FAR 52.216-7, the 'Allowable Cost and Payment' clause. This clause is incorporated into cost-reimbursable and time-and-materials contracts and requires annual submission of incurred costs to establish final indirect rates."
  },
  {
    q: "What is a good analogy for the ICS?",
    a: "A tax return for government contracts. Just as a tax return reconciles estimated tax payments against what you actually owed, the ICS reconciles provisional billing rates against actual indirect costs incurred."
  },
  {
    q: "What is a provisional billing rate?",
    a: "A pre-approved estimated indirect rate used to calculate and invoice indirect costs on government contracts throughout the fiscal year. It is temporary — a placeholder until actual costs are known after year-end."
  },
  {
    q: "What is a final indirect rate?",
    a: "An indirect rate computed after fiscal year-end using actual costs incurred and actual allocation bases. Final rates are permanent for the fiscal year they cover and are established through the ICS process."
  },
  {
    q: "Who are the three main parties that use the ICS?",
    a: "DCAA (performs the audit or desk review), the Contracting Officer (approves final rates and processes billing adjustments), and the contractor's accounting team (prepares the submission and responds to inquiries)."
  },
  {
    q: "What happens if the provisional overhead rate was 85% but the final rate is 78%?",
    a: "The contractor overbilled overhead costs. For every dollar of direct labor, the contractor collected 85 cents in overhead but only earned 78 cents. The contractor must refund the 7-cent-per-dollar difference to the government."
  },
  {
    q: "Is 'ICE' the correct term for an Incurred Cost Submission?",
    a: "No. ICE (Incurred Cost Electronically) was an older DCAA submission tool. The correct term for the submission package itself is always ICS — Incurred Cost Submission."
  }
]} />

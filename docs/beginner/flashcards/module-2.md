  {
    q: "What is the dollar threshold that triggers an ICS filing requirement?",
    a: "Contractors must file when they have cost-reimbursable or time-and-materials contracts where total costs expected to be incurred exceed $250,000 in a fiscal year."
  },
  {
    q: "When is the ICS due for a contractor with a calendar fiscal year (January 1 – December 31)?",
    a: "June 30 — six months after the fiscal year ends on December 31."
  },
  {
    q: "What is the general rule for calculating the ICS deadline?",
    a: "The ICS is due six months after the end of the contractor's fiscal year, regardless of when that fiscal year ends."
  },
  {
    q: "What can DCAA do if a contractor files late?",
    a: "DCAA can recommend that the contracting officer withhold 5% of amounts invoiced on cost-type contracts until the ICS is submitted."
  },
  {
    q: "Does a Firm-Fixed-Price (FFP) contract trigger an ICS filing requirement?",
    a: "No. FFP contracts do not involve reimbursement of actual indirect costs and do not trigger an ICS filing requirement. Only cost-reimbursable and time-and-materials contracts trigger the obligation."
  },
  {
    q: "Do Time-and-Materials (T&M) contracts trigger an ICS filing requirement?",
    a: "Yes. T&M contracts bill labor hours at loaded rates that include indirect cost components. They trigger the ICS filing requirement just as cost-reimbursable contracts do."
  },
  {
    q: "What makes a submission 'adequate' in DCAA's view?",
    a: "An adequate submission includes all required schedules, a properly signed contractor certification (Schedule H), internally consistent data, and indirect rates that can be computed from the information presented."
  }
]} />
